Managing email accounts and maintenance procedures across multiple domains and organizations.

## Email Maintenance Procedure

Context: Need a regular process to keep email accounts clean, secure, and organized across multiple domains.

## Monthly Email Maintenance Checklist

**Security Review:**
- [ ] Review recent account activity
  - Check [Google Account Activity](https://myaccount.google.com/notifications)
  - Look for suspicious sign-ins or devices
- [ ] Update passwords (quarterly)
  - Use password manager
  - Update in [cat2net users](https://docs.google.com/spreadsheets/d/1LdyZlFieSd_1APTbG0QahfwZgqBaA9PigO9_5SPSkmk/edit?gid=1959043129#gid=1959043129)
- [ ] Review connected apps
  - [Google Account Permissions](https://myaccount.google.com/permissions)
  - Remove unused third-party access

**Storage Management:**
- [ ] Check storage usage
  - [Google One Storage](https://one.google.com/storage)
  - Target: Keep under 80% capacity
- [ ] Clean up large emails
  - Search: `has:attachment larger:10M`
  - Archive or delete old attachments
- [ ] Empty trash and spam
  - Trash auto-deletes after 30 days
  - Spam auto-deletes after 30 days

**Organization:**
- [ ] Process inbox to zero (or near-zero)
  - Archive processed emails
  - Delete unnecessary emails
  - Create/update labels as needed
- [ ] Review filters and rules
  - [Gmail Filters](https://mail.google.com/mail/u/0/#settings/filters)
  - Update or remove outdated filters
- [ ] Update email signatures
  - Keep contact info current
  - Check across all accounts

## Quarterly Email Audit

**Every 3 months:**
- [ ] Review all email accounts in inventory
- [ ] Verify forwarding rules still needed
- [ ] Check for accounts that can be closed
- [ ] Update documentation
- [ ] Review storage costs (Google One, etc.)

**Schedule:**
- Q1: January (after tax season prep)
- Q2: April (after tax filing)
- Q3: July (mid-year review)
- Q4: October (before year-end)
